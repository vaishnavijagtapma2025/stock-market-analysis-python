# Starter Idea - Sanitation And Child Nutrition

## Why This Is Interesting

This connects household infrastructure to child well-being, which makes it policy-relevant and easy to motivate. It also works well as a careful descriptive project.

## Likely Project Type

Descriptive

## Core Question

How do sanitation conditions and child nutrition outcomes move together across places or groups?

## Good Stakeholder

A public health team, nutrition mission unit, or district planning office.

## Likely Main Metric

Your main metric could be:

- difference in stunting, wasting, or underweight rates across sanitation categories
- or a simple association coefficient if that fits your design

## Simple Baseline

Start with a very simple comparison:

- households with improved sanitation versus households without it
- or states above and below the median sanitation rate

## Likely Data Sources

- NFHS sanitation variables
- NFHS child nutrition variables

## First 24-Hour Feasibility Check

- confirm that both sanitation and nutrition variables are available in the same dataset
- make one simple grouped summary table

## Main Risk

This topic can tempt teams into causal claims that the data cannot support. If you keep it descriptive and honest, it can still be a very strong project.

## How To Keep It Small

Start with:

- one sanitation measure
- one nutrition outcome
- one geography level

That keeps the project clear and finishable.
