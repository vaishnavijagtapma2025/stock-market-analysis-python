# Starter Idea - Women's Digital Access Gap

## Why This Is Interesting

Digital access shapes financial inclusion, information access, and economic opportunity. This project is easier to scope than it first sounds and can still produce a strong result.

## Likely Project Type

Descriptive

## Core Question

How large are gender gaps in digital access across Indian states or districts?

## Good Stakeholder

A digital inclusion team, women and child development department, or nonprofit working on gender equity.

## Likely Main Metric

Your main metric could be:

- percentage-point gap in phone ownership
- percentage-point gap in internet use
- or a simple combined access index

## Simple Baseline

Your baseline can simply be:

- the overall average gap
- or the national average compared with each state

## Likely Data Sources

- NFHS indicators on phone ownership, internet use, bank access, and related measures

## First 24-Hour Feasibility Check

- confirm that the variables exist in the version of NFHS you plan to use
- pick one level of geography
- make one quick state comparison table

## Main Risk

Teams sometimes turn a descriptive project into a causal claim without the data to support it. Do not do that unless you truly redesign the project and get approval.

## How To Keep It Small

Start with:

- one or two digital access measures
- one geography level
- one comparison structure

Clarity matters more than breadth here.
